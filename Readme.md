# AI Governance Policy Framework for Oil & Gas

## Objective
Establish rules for responsible AI use in oil and gas operations, including exploration analytics, predictive maintenance, pipeline monitoring, refinery optimization, HSE reporting, procurement, and corporate productivity.

## Scope
Applies to employees, contractors, vendors, and business units using AI systems, machine learning models, generative AI, computer vision, or automated decision support.

## AI Risk Tiers
| Tier | Example | Required Governance |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Drafting meeting notes | User review, no confidential data unless approved |
| Moderate | Maintenance trend analysis | Data owner approval, validation, logging |
| High | Pipeline anomaly prioritization | Human-in-the-loop, OT safety review, model monitoring |
| Critical | Automated shutdown recommendation | Executive approval, safety engineering validation, formal change management |

## Policy Requirements
- AI systems must have a named business owner, technical owner, and data owner.
- AI use cases must be inventoried before production use.
- Sensitive data, geologic data, production data, employee data, and vendor confidential information must be protected.
- AI outputs that affect safety, production, trading, legal, or regulatory decisions require human review.
- High-risk models must be tested for accuracy, bias, drift, explainability, security, and resilience.
- AI vendors must complete vendor risk review before onboarding.
- AI-generated code, procedures, or operational instructions must be reviewed before use.

## Oil & Gas AI Use Case Approval Checklist
- Business purpose documented
- Asset or process impacted identified
- Data classification completed
- Safety impact reviewed
- OT/ICS impact reviewed
- Cybersecurity review completed
- Vendor review completed if external AI is used
- Monitoring and rollback plan defined
